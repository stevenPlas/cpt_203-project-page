Balancing your Life.

Author: Steven Plas
Date: 10-29-2016

How do you balance work, life and school? This is a great question, and it is something that I
strive to achieve every single day. Is it easy? No. Is it possible? Yes, it is possible, but it is not an easy accomplishment.
Over the years, I have tried everything from completely focusing on one thing, then switch to another, to balancing everything at the same time.
What has worked for me? I think I need to do some explaining about myself first,
then we will go into how to balance your life, for the better.


I work full time, I am a full time student, and I have a full time girlfriend, on top of all of my friends. When it comes to balancing these, it is a hard feat to accomplish. For starters, working full time means that I have to be at my place of work 40 hours a week,
No if’s and’s or but’s about it. When you figure out the math, there is 168 hours in a week, 168-40 = 128. So after work I have a total, of 128 hours for
everything left over. For school, I spend a total of 8 hours a week, just in class, and that takes that 128 hours down to 120. That is just the time IN class,
not actual homework. When you figure out the homework, I spend about 30 hours a week on homework, so that knocks that 120 down to 90 hours a week. After that,
you have to figure in the amount of sleep you get. I manage to sleep about seven hours and thirty minutes a night, from 11:00pm, to 6:30am. That calculates
out to 52.5 hours of sleep per week. Now I am down to 37.5 hours of my life, and then there is the commute! When you figure out that I spend 30 minutes
traveling one way to school, it adds up to three more hours of just driving to school! Now I’m left with 34.5 hours for the rest of my week.
I am not counting in the commute to my place of work, because it is about a mile away from where I live, so a minute here or there doesn’t matter too much in this area.

Let’s talk about how to balance all of this. First, you need to figure out how much time you spend on everything.
Much like I have in the last portion of this post, and then you need to figure out how to make it work for YOUR life. Everyone’s life is different, I work a
different shift, and go to school at a different time than what someone else will. So this will be different for each and every person,
but it is something that will work, no matter who you are, or what you do, or how busy you are.

“Balance” What do you mean by balance? I mean exactly what it says. You must balance your life, according to your life. But don’t over-do it.
The strongest tool that anyone has in their pocket at all times, is their cell phone. You can do multiple things with your cell phone, but there is
one thing that people don’t use it for, and that is a schedule, or daily planner, a notepad, anything that lets you keep track, and write things down.
You want to use that to your advantage, use reminders, use everything at your disposal, in order to keep track of your life, and balance it.

You want to sit down, and figure out what all you have to accomplish for the week, and figure out how many hours a day you will need to spend on it
in order to accomplish said goal. Now you may be wondering, “Why do a little bit daily, instead of all at once?” Because you will burn out.
If you don’t separate things, and use up that last 34.5 hours a little each day, then you will go insane.
You will break. Everything will slowly come crashing down, because when you do too much work, and not any fun time at all, then you begin to slowly,
but surly, hate your life, all because you’re not having any fun. If you do not spread out your work, and spend a little time doing what you want,
or with people you love and care about, then you are going to drive yourself crazy.

It is without a doubt in my mind that this method works, it is the only reason that I have come as far as I have.
It may have taken me a while to learn this, but it works. Just keep a daily schedule of what needs done, and make some free time for yourself.
It is the only way to live, when you are so busy with life.
